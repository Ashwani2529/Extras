1. Bara Imambara

Bara Imambara | #1 of 5 Places to Visit in Lucknow

Bara Imambara, also known as Asafi Imambara, is an imambara complex in Lucknow, India, built by Asaf-ud-Daula, Nawab of Awadh in 1784. It is considered a marvel of engineering and a fine specimen of Mughal architecture. The imambara is primarily known for its incredible maze, known as Bhul Bhulaiya, located on the upper floor of the monument. It is said to be the world's largest structure that is unsupported by beams, and no wood or metal has been used in its construction.The imambara houses the graves of Nawab Asaf-ud-Daula and his wife Shamsunnisa Begum. Muslims perform their sacred rituals during the Muharram festival, also known as Azadari in the local language

2. British Residency

British Residency | #2 of 5 Places to Visit in Lucknow

The British Residency, also known as the Residency Complex or British Residency, is a group of several buildings in a common precinct in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India. It served as the residence for the British Resident General who was a representative in the court of the Nawab. Construction started during the rule of Nawab Asaf-ud-Daulah and ended during the rule of Nawab Saadat Ali Khan II, who was the fifth Nawab of the province of Awadh (British spelling Oudh). Construction took place between 1780 and 1800 AD. Between 1 July 1857 and 17 November 1857, the Residency was subject to the Siege of Lucknow, part of the Indian Rebellion of 1857. The Residency is located in the heart of the city, in the vicinity of other monuments like Shaheed Smarak, Tehri Kothi, and High Court Building.

3. Rumi Darwaza

Rumi Darwaza | #3 of 5 Places to Visit in Lucknow

The Rumi Darwaza, also known as the Turkish Gate, is an imposing gateway in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh, India.It was built by Nawab Asaf-Ud-Daula in 1784 and is an example of Awadhi architecture.It stands at an impressive height of 60ft and was modeled after the Sublime Porte (Bab-iHümayun) in Istanbul.The gateway once served as the entrance to Old Lucknow City.It is a fine example of Awadhi architecture and an amalgamation of Mughal and Awadhi architecture. Not only locals but also international people visit the place to admire the historical heritage.

4. Husainabad Clock Tower

Husainabad Clock Tower | #4 of 5 Places to Visit in Lucknow

Husainabad Clock Tower is a tall clock tower located in Lucknow, India.It was built in 1881 by Hussainabad Trust to mark the arrival of Sir George Couper, the first lieutenant governor of the United Province of Avadh.The tower is adjacent to the Rumi Darwaza, near to Bara Imambara and Teele Wali Masjid.It was designed by Richard Roskell Bayne and Rascal Payne, and is considered one of the finest specimens of British architecture in India.The tower has a 14 feet long pendulum and a dial in the shape of a 12 petalled flower.The hands of the clock in this belfry are made of gun metal, which were brought from Louisgate Hill in London.

5. Gomti Riverfront Park

Gomti Riverfront Park | #5 of 5 Places to Visit in Lucknow

Gomti Riverfront Park is a newly constructed park located on the banks of the Gomti River in Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh.It was inaugurated in October 2016 by the then Chief Minister, Mr. Akhilesh Yadav, with the aim of preserving and developing the river, which is considered as the lifeline of Uttar Pradesh.The park stretches over 2 km and has lush green surroundings in keeping with international standards.The main attraction of the park is the massive musical fountain, which can be seen from both sides of the river.The park is situated near Ambedkar Park and is one of the wonderful places to visit as part of Lucknow tour packages.
